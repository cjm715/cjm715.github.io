Chris Miles is a data scientist and machine learning engineer with a background in physics, applied mathematics, and scientific computing.

His work sits at the intersection of AI, computation, and mathematical modeling. Across industry and research settings, he has worked on machine learning, optimization, forecasting, simulation, and technical software, with Python as the main tool for building models and experiments.

He holds a Ph.D. in Physics and an M.S. in Applied Mathematics from the University of Michigan, and a B.S. in Physics from MIT. That training continues to shape the way he approaches engineering problems: start from the mechanism, make the assumptions explicit, and build models that are useful rather than decorative.

This site is where he writes about AI compute, physics, math, and side projects. Some posts are project walkthroughs, some are research-style notes, and some are engineering write-ups focused on how a system actually works under the hood.

Topics that show up often here:

- machine learning and optimization
- scientific computing and simulation
- mathematical models for physical systems
- technical software projects and experiments
- hardware-oriented ideas for learning systems
